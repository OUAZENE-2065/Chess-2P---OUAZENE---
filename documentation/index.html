Chess Between 2 Players

Created by: OUAZENE Abdemlohsen

Introduction

This documentation provides an overview of the "Chess Between 2 Players" application, including its features, installation instructions, and usage guidelines.

Features

  • Play chess between two players locally.
  • Interactive user interface.
  • Highlights valid moves for each piece.
  • Enforces standard chess rules (e.g., check, checkmate, castling).
  • Allows undoing moves.
  • Provides move history and a game timer.

Usage

Starting a Game

  1. Open the application.
  2. Click the "New Game" button to initialize the chess board.

Making a Move

  • Select a piece by clicking on it.
  • Valid moves will be highlighted on the board.
  • Drag and drop the piece to the desired valid position.

Undoing a Move

  • Click the "Undo" button to revert the last move.

How to Play

Objective

The main objective in chess is to checkmate your opponent's king. This means the king is in a position to be captured ('in check') and there is no legal move that the player can make to escape the threat of capture on the next move.

Game Setup

At the start of the game, the chessboard is laid out with each player's pieces set up in the following positions:

  • Back row (from left to right): Rook, Knight, Bishop, Queen, King, Bishop, Knight, Rook
  • Front row: Pawns

Piece Movement

  • King: Moves one square in any direction.
  • Queen: Moves any number of squares in any direction.
  • Rook: Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
  • Bishop: Moves any number of squares diagonally.
  • Knight: Moves in an L-shape: two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular.
  • Pawn: Moves one square forward (two squares on its first move); captures diagonally.

Special Moves

  • Castling: A special move involving the king and a rook. The king moves two squares towards the rook, and the rook moves to the square next to the king.
  • En Passant: A special pawn capture that can occur immediately after a pawn moves two squares forward from its starting position.
  • Promotion: When a pawn reaches the opponent's back rank, it is promoted to another piece (typically a queen).

End of the Game

The game can end in several ways:

  • Checkmate: One player's king is in check and there is no legal move to escape.
  • Stalemate: The player to move has no legal moves and their king is not in check.
  • Draw: The game can also end in a draw by mutual agreement, insufficient material to checkmate, or the fifty-move rule (no pawn moves or captures in the last fifty moves).

Chess Rules

The game follows the standard rules of chess. Here are some key points:

  • Each player takes turns to move one piece.
  • The objective is to checkmate the opponent's king.
  • Special moves such as castling, en passant, and pawn promotion are supported.
